About CSI
Baton Rouge

We are the local chapter of the Construction Specifications Institute — a national nonprofit that unifies architects, engineers, contractors, and product manufacturers around clear, consistent construction documentation.

CSI Certifications

CSI offers industry-recognized certifications that demonstrate your expertise in construction specifications and documentation. Our chapter members can help connect you with study resources.

CDT®

Construction Documents Technologist

The foundational CSI certification — demonstrates knowledge of the construction process, project delivery, and documentation. Great for early-career professionals.

Learn More →
CCS®

Certified Construction Specifier

For experienced specifiers — validates your ability to produce project specifications and documents that meet industry standards.

Learn More →
CCCA®

Certified Construction Contract Administrator

Demonstrates expertise in managing contracts during construction — ideal for architects and engineers involved in contract administration.

Learn More →
CCPR®

Certified Construction Product Representative

Designed for product representatives and manufacturer reps who need to demonstrate their knowledge of construction processes and documentation.

Who We Are

About CSI
Baton Rouge

We are the local chapter of the Construction Specifications Institute — a volunteer-led organization dedicated to advancing construction knowledge and professional standards across South Louisiana.

Our Mission
"To bring together the construction community in Baton Rouge — architects, engineers, contractors, and product representatives — to advance knowledge, improve documentation standards, and build professional relationships that make every project better."

The CSI Baton Rouge Chapter is one of nearly 100 local chapters of the Construction Specifications Institute across North America. We serve the greater Baton Rouge metro area, bringing national-level education and resources to the local construction community.

Our programs are open to anyone in the construction industry — from seasoned specifiers and architects to product representatives, contractors, and students exploring the field. We meet regularly throughout the year for evening programs, product presentations, and networking events.

Who We Serve

  • Architects & Interior Designers
  • Civil & Structural Engineers
  • Construction Specifiers
  • General & Specialty Contractors
  • Product Representatives & Manufacturers
  • Students & Early-Career Professionals
  • Owners, Developers & Facility Managers

What We Do

  • Monthly evening programs with product and technical presentations
  • CEU / learning unit credits at qualifying events
  • Certification exam support and study connections
  • Networking with local construction professionals
  • Access to the national CSI member community

Common Questions

Membership FAQ

Do I have to be a specifier to join CSI?

Not at all. CSI membership is open to anyone involved in the construction industry — architects, engineers, contractors, product representatives, owners, students, and more. If you work in construction, there's a place for you in CSI.

What's included in the national membership?

National CSI membership includes access to the CSI Learning Library, the Construction Specifier magazine, the CSI-Connect online community, discounted certification exam fees, and the national member directory. Local chapter membership adds access to our Baton Rouge programs and network.

How often does the chapter meet?

We typically host several evening programs throughout the year — usually held at Drusilla Seafood on Drusilla Lane in Baton Rouge at 5:30 PM. Dates vary, so subscribing to the newsletter is the best way to stay informed.

Can I attend an event before joining?

Yes! Non-members are welcome to attend chapter events. It's a great way to experience what CSI Baton Rouge is about before committing to membership. Reach out through the contact page and we'll add you to the guest list.

How do I renew my membership?

Renewals are handled annually. Members will be notified via the chapter newsletter. You can also reach out directly through the contact page or email us at [email protected].

Ready to Join?

Reach out and we'll get you set up. We're happy to answer any questions about what membership looks like for you.
